JobTrack: A Job Hunting Visualization and Management Tool

Problem Description

For many college students and recent graduates, job hunting is not only about finding job postings. It also involves managing a large amount of information, including deadlines, application status, interview progress, job descriptions, contact details, and follow-up tasks. In reality, this information is often spread across multiple places such as LinkedIn, Handshake, email, spreadsheets, calendars, and personal notes. Because the process is fragmented, students can easily miss deadlines, forget important details, or struggle to understand how their overall job search is going.

Some existing tools already try to support parts of this process. Handshake helps students search for internships and jobs, while LinkedIn provides features like saved jobs and a job tracker. Other tools such as Huntr also help users organize applications, notes, and deadlines. However, these tools mainly focus on job discovery or basic tracking rather than helping users clearly visualize the bigger picture of their search. This creates an information problem: students may have access to opportunities, but they still lack a simple way to organize, interpret, and reflect on their full job search process. Our project addresses this gap by exploring how a web app can combine management and visualization to help users feel more organized and in control.

App Description

JobTrack is a job search management web app designed mainly for college students and early-career job seekers who are looking for internships, part-time jobs, and full-time jobs. These users often have to manage job information from many different places, such as LinkedIn, Handshake, company websites, email, calendars, and personal notes. This creates an information problem because important details like deadlines, application status, job requirements, salary, location, and visa sponsorship can become scattered and difficult to compare.

JobTrack solves this problem by giving users one organized workspace for the job hunting process. Users can search and filter jobs by company, role, location, salary, experience level, and visa sponsorship. They can save job opportunities, add new jobs manually, and update each job as it moves through different stages, such as saved, applied, interview, offer, or rejected. This makes the tracking feature more than a simple list because users can actively add, edit, sort, and manage their own job search data.

The app also includes a job detail workspace for each saved opportunity. In this workspace, users can review key information about the role, write personal notes, record deadlines, and keep track of next-step tasks such as preparing for an interview, sending a follow-up email, or completing an application. This helps users treat job hunting as an ongoing process instead of a set of disconnected tasks.

Another major feature is the analytics page. This feature is not just a static visualization. The user selects a company and position from a sample database, and the page shows data related to that specific choice, including total applications, interview rate, offer rate, education distribution, location distribution, and visa sponsorship information. This helps users compare opportunities more clearly and make better decisions about which jobs to prioritize.

A typical user would first create an account or log in, then enter job preferences such as preferred role, location, and sponsorship needs. After that, the user can search for jobs, save opportunities, add jobs manually, move applications through the tracker, and open the detail workspace to manage notes and tasks. Finally, the user can use the analytics page to compare companies and roles before deciding which opportunities deserve more attention.

Overall, JobTrack reduces stress and confusion by turning scattered job search information into organized and actionable information. Instead of only storing job listings, the app helps users manage applications, understand patterns in their search, and decide what to do next.

Key Features

  • Job search and filtering by role, company, location, salary, experience level, and visa sponsorship
  • Application tracking system where users can add, edit, and update job records
  • Job detail workspace for notes, deadlines, status updates, and next-step tasks
  • Analytics page that uses company and position selectors to show interview rate, offer rate, education distribution, location distribution, and visa sponsorship data
  • Login, sign-up, dashboard, job board, application board, add-job form, analytics page, and settings page
