JobTrack: A Job Hunting Visualization and Management Tool

Problem Description

For many college students and recent graduates, job hunting is not only about finding job postings. It also involves managing a large amount of information, including deadlines, application status, interview progress, job descriptions, contact details, and follow-up tasks. In reality, this information is often spread across multiple places such as LinkedIn, Handshake, email, spreadsheets, calendars, and personal notes. Because the process is fragmented, students can easily miss deadlines, forget important details, or struggle to understand how their overall job search is going.

Some existing tools already try to support parts of this process. Handshake helps students search for internships and jobs, while LinkedIn provides features like saved jobs and a job tracker. Other tools such as Huntr also help users organize applications, notes, and deadlines. However, these tools mainly focus on job discovery or basic tracking rather than helping users clearly visualize the bigger picture of their search. This creates an information problem: students may have access to opportunities, but they still lack a simple way to organize, interpret, and reflect on their full job search process. Our project addresses this gap by exploring how a web app can combine management and visualization to help users feel more organized and in control.

App Description

Our web is a job hunting platform designed mainly for college students who are looking for internships, part-time jobs, and full-time jobs. Our primary users would be students who often feel overwhelmed by job information and unclear job requirements. Our users can use our website to organize job opportunities they find, search and filter jobs by location, salary, experience level, and visa sponsorship. In addition, the users can also use our website to see patterns in their search process and take notes. It will also analyze and show prioritization and visualization in the dedicated workspace. The main data in the data will come from users and the job opportunities’ information. This includes saved job information, search preferences, application status, notes, categories including location, salary, experience level, and sponsorship.

Our app will include features including job tracking and filtering systems, workspaces for notes and status updates, analysis and visualization features. First, the job tracking and filtering system lets users save opportunities, manage and sort them. Second, the workspace lets users record where they applied, deadlines, and personal impressions. Third, the analysis and visualization features summarizes search patterns. It also analyzes and presents the prioritization of job opportunities.

The app assists the users by reducing the stress and confusion they often face when job information is scattered across different platforms. With our app, users can organize opportunities in one place, compare them more clearly. This makes the process more manageable, especially for students balancing multiple aspects of work.
